Family Planning & Maternal Health

Q1. What are different kinds of family planning devices available in the National Family Welfare Programme?

  • Condoms (Nirodh)
  • IUCD – Cu-T 380A
  • Oral pills – Mala-N, Mala-D, Centchroman
  • Injectable contraceptive – Antara
  • Sterilization – Tubectomy, Vasectomy

Q2. What do you mean by cafeteria approach under NFWP?

  • Multiple contraceptive choices
  • Client chooses preferred method
  • Based on age and parity
  • Improves acceptability
  • Ensures informed choice

Q3. Who is an ideal candidate for Cu-T?

  • Married woman
  • At least one child
  • Wants spacing
  • No PID/STI
  • Not pregnant

Q4. Is it essential to know attitude of elderly regarding family planning?

  • Elders influence decisions
  • Cultural beliefs matter
  • Affects acceptance
  • Improves compliance
  • Prevents discontinuation

Q5. What is PNDT Act?

  • Pre-Natal Diagnostic Techniques Act
  • Prevents sex determination
  • Prevents female foeticide
  • Regulates diagnostic tests
  • Punishable offence

Q6. What are conventional contraceptives? What is an ideal contraceptive?

  • Condom
  • Diaphragm
  • Foams & jellies
  • Ideal: safe, effective
  • Reversible & acceptable

Q7. Enumerate high-risk pregnancy criteria.

  • Age <18 or >35 years

  • Previous obstetric complications
  • Anemia/PIH/Diabetes
  • Multiple pregnancy
